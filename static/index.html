Interpret ASL. In realtime.

Interpret American Sign Language on-the-go. Nueral network based accurate interpretations, multi-language support, no additoinal hardware or interenet connection required.

Approximately 7.5 million people in the United States have speech and hearing impairment.

However, only 0.75 to 1 million people actively use sign language as a mean of communication. This can be attributed to the fact that 98% of the people from the deaf-and-dumb community do not receive any education pertaining to the use of sign language in daily life and face intense social and cultural distaste. In order to bridge this communication gap, break the social stigma towards sign language and enable dynamic communication, we present our ASL interpretation app that uses deep learning technologies to translate ASL finger signs into text.

Interpret ASL finger-signs in real-time using a deep CNN.
Deploy the model in a multi-platform mobile application.
Make the mobile application as accessible and user-friendly as possible.
